Money & Banking

Currency: Taniti uses the U.S. Dollar (USD) as its official currency.

  • Many businesses also accept euros (EUR) and Japanese yen (JPY)
  • Major credit cards widely accepted
  • Several banks offer currency exchange services
  • ATMs available in Taniti City and major tourist areas

Language

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in tourist areas and city centers.

In rural areas and with older residents, English is less common. However, locals are friendly and patient with visitors. The hospital and major hotels have multilingual staff available.

Safety Information

üõ°Ô∏è Crime & Security

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. As tourism increases, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and petty crimes. Practice common sense precautions:

  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight
  • Use hotel safes for passports and extra cash
  • Be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas
  • Don't leave belongings unattended on beaches

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the power voltage?

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States. No adapter needed for US travelers.

What is the drinking age?

The drinking age is 18 years old. Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 AM.

What is the best time to visit?

Taniti has beautiful weather year-round! Peak season is December through March. For fewer crowds, consider visiting April-November.

Do I need a visa?

Visa requirements vary by country. Contact us for specific requirements based on your nationality.
